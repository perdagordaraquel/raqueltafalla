GROWTH AND YIELD PERFORMANCE OF SWEET PEPPER AS INFLUENCED BY ORGANIC AND IN0RGANIC FERTILIZER APPLICATION

CHAPTER 1

INTRODUCTION

Nature and Importance of the study

Sweet pepper,(Capsicum annum) of the Solanaceae, or nigthshade family, is grown throughout the world, and species not only includes the green bell-shaped, sweet peppers that are common garden varieties but also hot peppers used for spices such as chili. Most cultivated peppers belong to one and two major groups. The blossom yields the globose, mild - flavored,sweet peppers and the Spanish pepper.

Vegetable production ranks even higher than all root crops and grain legumes (Knott, 1967). Sweet pepper is a tropical and subtropical plant with a minimum temperature of 21°C with humidity of 70 to 75 percent however, a temperature of 30°C may reduce fruit set and cause buds and flowers to drop. The crop is rich in vitamins A and C, niacin, riboflavin and thiamine. Moderately deep, fertile, well-drained soil with medium nitrogen is a requirement for a succesful sweet pepper production (Bricked 1992)

Sweet pepper along with others Solanaceous crop such as eggplant, tomato and potato are cslled "warm season crops" because they can be grown under relatively warm climate. Pepper is apparently more drougth resistance than either tomato or eggplant. Nevertheless,best yield and a mean temparature at blossom setting time ranging from 18.48°C (Attfield, 1993). In sweet pepper, several investagations have already been undertake to determine the growth and yield response to fertilizer (Mercado, 1954; PCARRD, 1976; Knott and Deanon, 1970) but no studies had been made on the effect on the organic and inorganic fertilizer under Calbayog condition hence, this study.

Objective of the study

This study aimed to:

1. Evaluate the growth and yield performance of the sweet pepper as influenced by organic and inorganic fertilizer application under Calbayog condition.

2 Determine which of the treatments would gave the highest fruit yield under Calbayog condition.

3. Analyze the cost and return of growing sweet pepper under Calbayog condition.

Time and Place of the study

This study was conducted from September 30, 2005 up to February 14, 2006 at Brgy. Carayman Calbayog, City.

Scope and Limitation

This study was limited to the growth and yield performance of sweet pepper as influenced by inorganic using complete plus ammonphos fertilizer and organic fertilizer applications using chicken dung and carabao manure under Brgy. Carayman, Calbayog condition.
